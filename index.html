Course Overview

Algebra 1 develops algebraic reasoning through expressions, equations, inequalities, functions, systems, data, polynomials, quadratics, and mathematical modeling.

The course is designed as one reusable base course.

Final calendar, grading, completion, and academic record policies depend on the school implementing the course.

Learning Outcomes

  • Interpret algebraic expressions and rewrite them in equivalent forms.
  • Create and solve equations and inequalities from mathematical and contextual situations.
  • Represent and interpret linear functions using tables, graphs, equations, and verbal descriptions.
  • Solve systems of equations and inequalities and interpret their solutions.
  • Analyze data with scatter plots, lines of fit, and linear models.
  • Operate with exponent rules, scientific notation, polynomials, and introductory factoring.
  • Analyze introductory quadratic and exponential models and compare functions.
  • Use mathematical modeling to justify decisions clearly and responsibly.

Owlaris AI Use

  • Owlaris may explain concepts, ask diagnostic questions, give hints, create similar practice, and review reasoning.
  • Owlaris may not solve final projects, complete exams, or write answers for copying.
  • Students must submit their own reasoning and evidence.

Course Policies and Boundaries

  • Student-facing instruction is produced in Spanish; official standards names and codes may be preserved for traceability.
  • Students should show steps, explain reasoning, and verify answers when solving algebraic problems.
  • Calculators or graphing tools may be used when the task allows them, but students must still explain the mathematics.
  • Due dates, retake rules, grade thresholds, academic integrity procedures, and final records depend on the school implementing the course.
